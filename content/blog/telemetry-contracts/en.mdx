Defensive teams are flooded with data and still blind. The problem is rarely volume. It is **ambiguity**: events change shape across versions, vendors rename fields, and nobody owns the meaning of a record.

## What a contract is

A telemetry contract names:

- the **event** (what happened),
- the **entity** it is about,
- the **minimum fields** required to reason,
- and the **stability guarantees** you expect across releases.

Contracts are boring documents. That is why they work.

## From collection to comprehension

Collection without comprehension produces dashboards that feel insightful until an incident arrives. Contracts force you to decide what “known good” and “known suspicious” could look like **in your environment**—not in a vendor slide deck.

## A practical next step

Pick one high-risk workflow—remote access, privilege changes, identity federation—and write a one-page contract for the five signals you would want if you had to explain the system to a tired analyst at 03:00.

If you cannot write that page, your SIEM rules are built on hope.
