---
title: 'Attack surface'
shortDefinition: 'The set of reachable interfaces, identities, and trust edges an adversary can interact with.'
updatedAt: '2025-03-01'
---

Attack surface is not “how big the internet is.” It is a **boundary statement**: given a scope and a model of the system, what can be touched without already being trusted?

Good surface analysis is conservative. It prefers explicit lists (hosts, ports, identities, delegation edges) over vibes. When you expand the model, the surface expands—often suddenly.

---

## Practical use

When prioritizing fixes, compare surface reduction to control strength: shrinking reachable paths is often cheaper than perfecting monitoring on an enormous perimeter.
